My Experiences

Effect of a super-hydrophobic structure and its application on the car body to reduce drag. (2015- 2017)
Description and Results:
Different dimensions of vortex generators have been taken into consideration to study the influence of design parameters
The results show that vortex generators reduce drag.
By using these structures, drag coefficient is reduced up to 2%.
My Task:
For conducting this study, I used ANSYS Fluent Software to simulate the real condition. Based on previous literature results, More than 100 cases were tested. This project has been carried out under supervision of Dr. Moosavi.
MRI biomedical images analysis to quantify lumbar muscle changes before and six months after surgery. (2013-2014)
Description and Results:
Goal :By computer analysis of MRI images, quantify post-surgery alterations of lumbar muscle anatomy and contractile density.
Postoperative contractility variations for paraspinal muscles may deeply impact muscle activities, spinal loading, and stability.
My Task:
For patients suffering from low back pain, 3D-MRI Images of back muscles were acquired and saved in DICOM format.Then I imported these DICOM files into Mimics Software to perform image analysis, in order to extract quantitative characteristics of muscle contractility and anatomy (CSAS) before and after surgery.The measurements were also carried out on healthy volunteers. I implemented statistical comparisons of extracted CSAS data between two groups within a 7-month period. This project was supervised by Dr. Farahmand and Dr. Arjmand
